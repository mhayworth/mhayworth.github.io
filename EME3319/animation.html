Animation

My animation project has educational value to a beginner clarinet student learning his or her first scale. Moreover, it could be expanded to include scales in different keys as well as minor scales. Additionally, a teacher could customize the animation to a song that a student is having difficulty learning to help the student visualize how to move their hands. I think another music student that could benefit greatly from this style of animation is an aspiring pianist, as the keys are easy to see and follow.

While creating this animation project, as with many of Adobe's development tools, I find that a lot of the simple thing that I think should be easy to access are actually much harder to find. I had to look up how to rotate an image for my Clarinet metronome. It involved right click, digging through a menu, and then digging through a submenu to find the option to flip the image horizontally. Additionally, I found that when navigating to a different keyframe, it would automatically highlight (select) everything within that keyframe. I found this frustrating because in order to change one small thing, I had to click the keyframe I wanted, manually unselect everything, go back to the tool I wanted to use (i.e. text), and then make my changes.

Finally, one other feature I would like to see implemented into Adobe Animate to make the process feel better is automatically updating the selected keyframe instead of generating a new one. For example, when I wanted to change the text in one of my keyframes but clicked on the frame after it, it would automatically make a new keyframe next to the existing keyframe. I would like to see a toggle option to either generate a new keyframe or snap back to update the beginning keyframe in your selection.
