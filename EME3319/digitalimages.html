Digital Images

Event Flyers

The first event flyer I made is an informational flyer for my club, the society of software developers. Some design choices I made include the picture superiority effect, as demonstrated through the two images that make up the main part of the flyer. Another principle I use is the alignment of the binary along the left side of the image, which spells out the ASCII encoding for "SSD". This made for an interesting flyer that will make people want to visit the next club meeting.

The second flyer I made is supposed to be a notice that could be sent out to UF fans and students about the football game's rescheduling to Sunday due to Hurricane Ian. I included the empty space principle, specifically the rule of thirds, to make an open area (the football field) that is both visually appealing and puts extra attention on the text, which is telling the audience that the game has been moved. I also made sure to keep the signal-to-noise ratio as good as possible, with the text being brief but clear, to make sure that anyone who sees this flyer will quickly and easily read the text.

Logo

For my logo, I decided to create a fun version of the logo for my club, the Society of Software Developers. I used a circular logo in addition to a fun font to make the club look more approachable. I included contrast between the inner and outer circle, while repeating the color of the outer circle for some of the letters. Additionally, the horizontal alignment of "UF" and "SSD" show that they are two separate acronyms, "University of Florida", and "Society of Software Developers". Even though the letters are tilted to the shape of the circle, it's clear that these letters form two separate words, which makes the logo easier to understand.

Infographic

For my infographic, I used Canva to create an infographic that explains how to become a software engineer for incoming college students. This is why I specifically choose to put statistics about getting a degree first, to encourage a college student to stay in their school until they finish their degree program. Some design principles I used include repetition of the box with text that goes down a grayscale gradient, with the text in the final step being white to show the completion of a student's journey to becoming a software engineer. I also used proximity of the images to their respective sections, such as the graph in the degree section, to emphasize the importance of each point.
